# /swans — Sherlock, Wizard, Alien, Nerd, Surgeon

Five distinct expert minds dissect a topic, then collide to produce insight none could reach alone. The value is in the collision, not the individual views.

## Topic

$ARGUMENTS

## How This Works

Each lens has a **specific domain** it owns. No lens may drift into another's territory — overlap is noise. Each lens produces exactly **2 findings** — no more. If you can't identify 2 genuinely distinct findings for a lens, produce 1 and state that the lens has limited applicability to this topic. Never pad.

The synthesis at the end is the main event. The 5 lenses are raw material — the synthesis is the product.

## Step 1: Calibrate

Before analyzing, size the topic. This determines depth.

> **Topic:** [restate in one precise sentence — strip ambiguity]
>
> **Stakes:** [LOW: easily reversible | MEDIUM: meaningful cost to reverse | HIGH: irreversible or bet-the-company]
>
> **Core tension:** [the fundamental trade-off or uncertainty at the heart of this topic, in one sentence]

If you cannot identify a core tension, the topic may be too vague to analyze well. State this and ask for clarification before proceeding.

## Step 2: Five Lenses

Run each lens fully before moving to the next. Stay strictly within each lens's domain.

---

### S — SHERLOCK (Evidence & Reality)

**Domain:** What is actually true vs. what people assume is true. You follow evidence chains and expose the gap between narrative and reality.

**You are looking for:**
- What does the evidence actually show — not what people say it shows?
- What is conspicuously absent from the narrative? (The dog that didn't bark.)
- Where is the reasoning circular — conclusion assumed in the premise?
- What would change your mind? (If nothing would, the position isn't evidence-based.)

**You do NOT:** innovate, propose solutions, assess technical feasibility, or make recommendations.

**Output — exactly 2 findings:**

> **FINDING 1: [title]**
> [What the evidence actually shows, stated precisely. Include the specific data point, observation, or logical chain — not "research suggests" but the actual thing.]
> **Implication:** [The one thing this means that isn't obvious]
>
> **FINDING 2: [title]**
> [Same format]
>
> **The gap:** [One sentence — the single biggest disconnect between what people believe about this topic and what the evidence supports]

---

### W — WIZARD (Systems & Leverage)

**Domain:** How the pieces connect. You map the system — feedback loops, leverage points, timing windows, second-order effects. You are a strategist, not an inventor.

**You are looking for:**
- Where are the feedback loops (virtuous or vicious)?
- What is the highest-leverage intervention point — the place where small input creates disproportionate output?
- What are the second and third-order consequences that nobody is modeling?
- What does the timing landscape look like — what windows are opening or closing?

**You do NOT:** invent novel solutions (that's Alien), evaluate technical constraints (that's Nerd), or prioritize actions (that's Surgeon).

**Output — exactly 2 findings:**

> **FINDING 1: [title]**
> [The system dynamic you've identified — how pieces connect, what drives what, where the leverage sits. Be specific about the mechanism, not just the outcome.]
> **Leverage insight:** [How to use this dynamic — what to amplify, dampen, or redirect]
>
> **FINDING 2: [title]**
> [Same format]
>
> **Timing:** [One sentence — what is time-sensitive about this topic right now, or "No time pressure identified" if genuinely none]

---

### A — ALIEN (Frame Breaks & Paradigm Shifts)

**Domain:** You have never seen human conventions before. You question why the *category* exists, not how to optimize within it. You import solutions from completely unrelated domains and invert problems.

**You are looking for:**
- Why does this problem exist at all? What created the conditions for it?
- What would someone from a completely different industry/discipline see that insiders are blind to?
- What if the opposite of the current assumption is true?
- What constraint is everyone accepting as fixed that is actually movable?

**You do NOT:** investigate evidence (Sherlock), map systems (Wizard), check technical feasibility (Nerd), or make action plans (Surgeon). Your job is to break the frame, not build within it.

**Output — exactly 2 findings:**

> **FINDING 1: [title]**
> [The reframe or paradigm break — stated as a concrete alternative worldview, not a vague "what if." Include the specific analogy, inversion, or imported model and explain WHY it applies here.]
> **If true:** [What changes about how you'd approach the entire topic]
>
> **FINDING 2: [title]**
> [Same format]
>
> **The question nobody is asking:** [One sentence — the question that, if answered, would make most of the current debate irrelevant]

---

### N — NERD (Hard Constraints & Mechanics)

**Domain:** Technical truth. The actual numbers, constraints, failure modes, and mechanics that govern what is and isn't possible. You are the reality check on everything else.

**You are looking for:**
- What are the actual hard constraints (physics, math, resource limits, platform limits)?
- What breaks first under stress — and at what specific threshold?
- What technical detail, if known, would change the strategic conversation?
- Where is complexity hiding that will surface later?

**You do NOT:** strategize, innovate, investigate narratives, or prioritize. You state what is technically true and what the constraints actually are.

**Output — exactly 2 findings:**

> **FINDING 1: [title]**
> [The specific technical reality — with numbers, thresholds, or precise mechanical descriptions where applicable. Not "this could be slow" but "this hits O(n^2) at ~10K items because..."]
> **Constraint:** [What this makes impossible, expensive, or risky — stated as a hard boundary]
>
> **FINDING 2: [title]**
> [Same format]
>
> **The detail that changes everything:** [One technical fact that, if the other lenses knew it, would alter their conclusions]

---

### S — SURGEON (Decision Triage)

**Domain:** You convert analysis into decisions. You cut with precision — what to do, what to stop doing, what to defer, and in what order. You operate under the principle that a good decision now beats a perfect decision later.

**You are looking for:**
- What is the single most important decision to make right now?
- What decisions are reversible (experiment freely) vs. irreversible (measure twice)?
- What should we STOP doing or thinking about? (Elimination is as valuable as action.)
- What is the minimum viable next step that creates maximum information?

**You do NOT:** investigate, innovate, map systems, or assess technical constraints. You take the other lenses' input and convert it to action.

**Output:**

> **THE CUT:** [The single most important decision or action, stated in one sentence]
>
> **Decision type:** [REVERSIBLE: experiment and adjust | IRREVERSIBLE: validate before committing | PREMATURE: not enough information yet — here's what to learn first]
>
> **Do now:** [1-2 concrete actions, each with expected outcome]
>
> **Stop doing:** [1-2 things to eliminate — activities, assumptions, or debates that are consuming energy without producing value]
>
> **Defer:** [1-2 things that matter but not yet — with the trigger condition for when they become urgent]

---

## Step 3: Collision (The Main Event)

This is where the real insight lives. Do not summarize the lenses — synthesize them.

### Convergence
Where did 2+ lenses independently arrive at the same conclusion from different angles? These are your highest-confidence insights. State each as a single declarative sentence.

### Productive Tensions
Where do lenses genuinely disagree — not because one is wrong, but because there's a real trade-off? Name the trade-off precisely. Do NOT resolve it artificially — present the honest tension and what choosing each side costs.

### Emergent Insight
State one insight that **only becomes visible when you hold multiple lenses simultaneously** — something no single lens could have produced alone. This is the unique value of SWANS. If you cannot identify a genuine emergent insight, say so — do not fabricate one.

### Kill List
Name 1-3 things that the analysis revealed are **not worth worrying about** — topics, risks, or questions that seemed important going in but that the five-lens examination shows are distractions or already resolved. This is noise reduction.

### Verdict
> **One sentence:** [What to do, stated with conviction. Not hedged, not qualified. The best available answer given everything above.]
>
> **Confidence:** [HIGH: lenses converge, evidence strong | MEDIUM: reasonable case but genuine unknowns remain | LOW: high uncertainty, verdict is a best guess — here's what would raise confidence]

## Quality Gate

Before delivering, self-check:
- [ ] Every finding says something the reader didn't already know or assume
- [ ] No two findings across any lenses say the same thing in different words
- [ ] The emergent insight is genuinely emergent, not a restatement
- [ ] The kill list actually eliminates something the reader was probably worrying about
- [ ] The verdict is specific enough that someone could act on it without asking follow-up questions
- [ ] Total output is dense — every sentence carries information. Zero filler.

If any check fails, revise before delivering.
